What are viruses?

Viruses are microscopic parasites, generally much smaller than bacteria. They lack the capacity to thrive outside of a host body.
Predominantly, viruses have a reputation for being the cause of epidemics. Widespread events of diesease and deaths like, the 2009 H1N1 (swine flu) pandemic, the 2014 outbreak of Ebola in West Africa and the ongoing COVID-19 are the sure examples for this. While such viruses are surely foes for the scientists and medical professionals; other viruses are usful for them. They use viruses as research tools to understand the mechanics of protein synthesis in the virus itself.

Structure of a coronavirus:

Coronaviruses are a group of related viruses that cause dieseses in mammals and birds. In humans and birds, they cause respiratory tract infections that can range from mild to fatal. Mild illnesses in humans include common cold, while more fatal varieties can cause SARS, MERS and COVID-19.
Coronaviruses are large, roughly spherical viruses with unique surface projections. Their size is highly variable with average diameters of 80 to 120 nanometers. They are enclosed in an envelope encapsulated with a number of protein molecules. The viral envelope is made up of a lipid bilayer in which the membrane, envelope and spike structured proteins are anchored. This protects the virus when its outside the host cell. Th spikes are the most distinguishing features of coronavirus and are responsible for the crown like surface, and hence the name corona. On an average a coronavirus has 74 spikes. Each spike is about 20 nanometers long and is composed of a trimer of the spike protein. Inside the envelope, there is the nucleocapsid, which is formed from multiple copies of the nucleocapsid protein.

Effects of coronaviruses in the past:

COVID-19 is not the only epidemic caused by the coronavirues. Actually, these viruses have a very dreadful history. HUman coronavirus was first invented in the mid-1960s. From then, seven types of coronaviruses have been found that can infect humans. These are:

  1. 229E (alpha coronavirus)
  2. NL63 (alpha coronavirus)
  3. OC43 (beta coronavirus)
  4. HKU1 (beta coronavirus)
  5. MERS-CoV (beta coronavirus that causes Middle East Respiratory Syndrome)
  6. SARS-CoV (beta coronavirus that causes Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome)
  7. SARS-CoV-2 or 2019-nCoV(novel coronavirus that causes coronavirus diesease 2019, or COVID-19)

People around the world get commonly affected with human coronaviruses 229E, NL63, OC43 and HKU1.
But sometimes, coronaviruses that infect animals evole and make peole sick. The recent examples are MERS-CoV, SARS-CoV and 2019-nCoV.
The SARS-CoV virus is a strain of coronavirus that causes Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome which infects the epithelial cells within the lungs. It infects humans, bats and palm civets. It was first identified in the year 2003 and caused a major outbreak of SARS diesease at that time.
The MERS-CoV virus is the type of type of coronavirus that causes Middle East Respiratory Syndrome which is a viral respiratory diesease. It was first identified in Saudi Arabia in the year 2012, and caused a lot of havoc in the whole world. Approximately 35% of the the reported patients died with the MERS diesease.
So, we see, that the COVID-19 is not the only coronavirus member that has caused so much panic and devastation, there are other members too that have caused upheavel in the human lives in the past.

Symptoms of COVID-19 diesease:

Most common symptoms:

  • Fever
  • Dry Cough
  • Tiredness

Less common symptoms:

  • Aches and pains
  • Sore Throat
  • Diarrhoea
  • Conjunctivitis
  • Loss of taste or smell or both
  • A rash on skin, or discolouration of fingers and toes

Serious symptoms:

  • Difficulty in breathing or shortness of breathe
  • Pain in chest
  • Loss of speech or movement

Seek immediate medical attention if you have any of these symptoms. On average it takes 5-6 days from when someone is infected with the virus for symptoms to show, however it can take up to 14 days.

Also the people who take drugs, the ladies who are in their pregnancy, the elderly, the children and the people with weak immune systems can be affected much easily. Virus mutation and its impact on the rate of infection and mortality

A mutation is a change in a DNA sequence. Mutations can result from DNA copying mistakes made during cell division, exposure to ionizing radiation, or exposure to chemicals called mutagens.
The mutations in all the replicating viruses, including coronavirus enhance the ability of viral infection as well as an escape from host immune attack. It is also found that in the countries which have found a new strain of the virus, the mortality rate is more. So, it sums up that, virus mutation affects the mortality rate.

Preparation for upcoming pandemics:

Preparation on gobal scale:
The countries should develop the capability to produce new vaccines, drugs and rapid diagnostics for new pathogens within months, and not years. They should also pioneer a strong and sustainable global health security system. They should build a deployable clinical capability for international infectious diesease emergencies. They should also invest more in a robust, highly capable national public health system that can manage the challenges of pandemic response. They should develop a national plan to harness effectively all healthcare assets in a catastrophic pandemic. The countries implement an international strategy for addressing research that increases pandemic risks. At last, they should ensure the national security community is well prepared to prevent, detect and respond to infectious-disease emergencies.

Preparation on local scale:
The citizens should be provided adequate information about the pandemic. They should be made aware of all the preventive measures and that they should not panic. In that way, they will feel safe for themselves.

Difference between an epidemic and a pandemic:

An epidemic is a diesease that affects a large number of people within a community, population or region.
Whereas, a pandemic is an epidemic thatspreads over multiple countries or continents. It is said to be a travelling epidemic.
